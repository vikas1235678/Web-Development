Rains in Chandigarh as 13 states brace for stormy weather, thunderstorms

Light rains were witnessed in Chandigarh and nearby areas, along with strong winds and an overcast sky, on Monday morning as north India braced for a stormy weather, reported news agency IANS. Authorities in Haryana and Punjab remained on alert following a storm advisory issued by the Meteorological department. Taking a precautionary measure, the Haryana government has ordered all schools to remain closed on Monday and Tuesday. Besides this, few private schools in Chandigarh have also declared holiday for two days. The Met department has predicted thunderstorms, accompanied by strong winds and rain, in Haryana and Punjab from Monday to Wednesday. Other states in the neighbourhood are also on alert. Last week, more than 100 people were killed across north India due to damages caused by dust storms. In Uttar Pradesh, alerts have been issued for Rampur, Moradabad, Gaziabad, Meerut and other districts of western Uttar Pradesh. Meanwhile, the maximum temperatures at most places in Punjab, Haryana and Chandigarh remained between 31.5 to 38.5 degrees Celsius on Sunday, with rainfall at some places and overcast sky at several in others. On Sunday, the Union Home Ministry said thunderstorms, accompanied by squall, hail and heavy rains, are likely to occur in at least 13 states and two union territories. Thunderstorms are expected at isolated places over Jammu and Kashmir and Himachal Pradesh, while thunderstorm accompanied with gusty winds are expected on Monday in Uttarakhand and Punjab. The Met department has also predicted dust storm in Rajasthan. Besides this, heavy rains are likely on Monday in isolated places over Assam, Meghalaya, Nagaland, Manipur, Mizoram and Tripura, a Home Ministry official said on Sunday, quoting an Met department advisory.
